A Useful Lie of Scale

If you shrank the Sun to the size of a basketball, then…

Earth would be
2.2 mm
A grain of sand, sitting 26 metres away. Smaller than a pinhead at the end of a basketball court.
Jupiter would be
2.5 cm
About the size of a marble. Sitting roughly a football field away — 135 metres from your basketball.
Neptune would be
9 mm
A small bead, sitting almost a kilometre away — at the far end of a long park.
Nearest star
7,000 km
Proxima Centauri. The next basketball is on the other side of the planet.
